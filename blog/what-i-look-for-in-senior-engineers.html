Engineering Management

What I Look For In Senior Engineers

Senior engineering is not just harder tickets. It is a broader operating radius, better judgment, clearer communication, and systems that scale beyond one person.

When I coach engineers toward senior scope, I pay attention to how their work changes the environment around them. A strong engineer can solve a difficult problem. A senior engineer makes a class of problems easier for everyone else to solve.

Signals I Trust

  • They reduce ambiguity. They can turn a vague problem into options, tradeoffs, risks, and a path forward.
  • They create leverage. Their work produces reusable patterns, documentation, tools, or decisions that help other engineers move faster.
  • They communicate early. They surface blockers, explain risk, and keep stakeholders oriented before surprises become emergencies.
  • They ship in useful increments. They avoid polishing optional complexity before the core value is proven.
  • They raise the floor. Their standards improve the team without requiring them to personally review every detail forever.

Documentation Is A Leadership Skill

Documentation is not busywork. It is how individual work becomes organizational capability. Runbooks, examples, decision records, diagrams, and migration notes are force multipliers when they help teammates operate independently.

What Changes At The Next Level

The next level is usually less about raw technical ability and more about judgment under ambiguity. Senior engineers understand when to simplify, when to challenge scope, when to build a platform, when to delete complexity, and when to slow down enough to protect the system.
